## ARTICLE 3: The Workforce System Isn't Failing — It's Misaligned (Mar 23)

### LinkedIn (Day 0)
The U.S. spends $3.6 billion annually on workforce development through WIOA.

87% of employers still report difficulty filling positions.

Only 37% of workforce trainees are employed in their trained field one year later.

These numbers aren't contradictory. They're diagnostic. The system isn't failing — it was never aligned.

Training providers design curricula based on what they can deliver. Employers post requirements based on ideal candidates. Job seekers navigate between disconnected providers. Everyone is working. Nothing connects.

I mapped where the system breaks — and the three shifts that would change it.

https://barlow.app/posts/workforce-system-misaligned

### X/Twitter (Day 0)
$3.6B spent on workforce development yearly. 87% of employers still can't find talent.

https://barlow.app/posts/workforce-system-misaligned

### Instagram Caption (Day 1)
"We don't have a skills gap. We have a pipeline gap."

$3.6 billion spent annually on workforce development. Employers still say they can't find talent. Trainees still can't find jobs in their field.

The disconnect isn't about training quality. It's about the handoffs — from data to curriculum, from training to placement, from placement to retention.

Every handoff is a place where the system breaks. And nobody owns the space between.

Full breakdown on the blog. Link in bio.

#WorkforceDevelopment #SystemsThinking #Detroit #Leadership #FutureOfWork

### LinkedIn Follow-Up (Day 3)
One detail from the workforce piece that keeps coming up in conversations:

The question we measure — "did they complete the program?" — is the wrong question.

The right question: "Are they still employed 18 months later? In what? At what wage?"

Most workforce systems can't answer that. Not because they don't care — because the data infrastructure doesn't exist to track outcomes past the program boundary.

That's the system gap. Not skills. Not motivation. Data architecture.

## ARTICLE 5: Why Your Network Isn't Working (Previously Published)

### LinkedIn (Day 0)
You have 2,000 LinkedIn connections. You went to 40 events last year. You have contacts in every industry.

So why doesn't your network produce anything?

Because you're treating it like a Rolodex instead of infrastructure.

A real network has three things: context (why you're connected), cadence (when you engage), and clear value exchange (what you each bring).

Without those, you just have a list.
